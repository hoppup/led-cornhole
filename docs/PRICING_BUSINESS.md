## 1. Market Pricing — What Cornhole Boards Sell For (No LEDs)

| Tier | Price Range | Construction | Examples |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Budget** | $50–100 | Particle board / thin plywood, pine frame | Walmart, Amazon basics |
| **Mid-range** | $150–250 | 1/2"–3/4" birch, decent frame | Etsy sellers, local builders |
| **Premium** | $250–400 | 3/4" Baltic Birch, hardwood frame, CNC cut, UV print | Cornhole Solutions (~$300), SlickWoodys (~$250-350), Skip's Garage (~$200-350) |
| **Tournament** | $350–500+ | 3/4" Baltic Birch, poplar frame, ACL certified, pro finish | Cornhole Pro MN (~$400-500), WSOC official boards |
| **With LEDs (no scoreboard)** | +$30–50 | Basic LED ring/edge lights, no scoreboard | Various small sellers |
| **With LED Scoreboard** | $600–1,000 | Full Bluetooth scoreboard + app | YoreBoard |

**Key takeaway:** Premium boards without any electronics sell for $250-400. Add a built-in LED scoreboard and you're in a category almost nobody occupies between $400-1,000.

## 3. Shipping Analysis

### The Problem
Cornhole boards are **big and heavy** — the #1 challenge for online sales.

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Box dimensions | 48" × 24" × 8" (boards stacked face-to-face) |
| Package weight | ~55-65 lbs (two 27-30 lb boards + padding) |
| Dimensional weight | 48×24×8 ÷ 139 = **66 lbs** (FedEx/UPS charge whichever is higher) |
| Combined dimensions | 48+24+8 = 80" (under 130" limit, no oversized surcharge) |

### Shipping Costs (Estimated from Central Kansas)

| Carrier / Service | Regional (~500 mi) | Cross-country (~2,000 mi) | Notes |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| **FedEx Ground** (retail) | $70-90 | $100-140 | |
| **FedEx Ground** (PirateShip discount) | $45-60 | $70-95 | **Use this** — saves 30-50% |
| **UPS Ground** (retail) | $65-85 | $95-130 | |
| **UPS Ground** (PirateShip discount) | $40-55 | $65-90 | |
| **USPS** | Not viable | Not viable | Too heavy/large for USPS |

### Shipping Strategy

**Keep it under 50 lbs per box when possible:**
- The 50 lb threshold triggers extra handling surcharges (~$25)
- Ship bags separately in a small flat-rate box (~$10) if it helps keep board box under 50 lbs
- Consider lighter packaging (no foam — use cardboard corners and kraft paper)

**Recommended shipping setup:**
- **PirateShip.com** — free to use, gives you commercial FedEx/UPS rates (30-50% off retail)
- FedEx Ground is usually cheapest for large/heavy items
- Central Kansas is actually a great shipping location — you're roughly centered in the US, so max distance to anywhere is ~1,500 miles

**Flat-rate shipping pricing:**
- Charge a **flat $75 shipping** anywhere in the continental US
- Most orders will cost you $45-70 to ship, so you break even or make a small margin
- "Free shipping" works too — just bake $75 into the board price

### Electronics Kit Shipping (Easy)
- Small box: 10" × 8" × 4", under 2 lbs
- **USPS First Class or Priority:** $8-12 anywhere in the US
- This is the product to sell online — cheap to ship, good margins

## 4. Where to Sell

### Phase 1: Local (Start Here)

| Channel | Effort | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Facebook Marketplace** | Low | Free listings, huge reach in Kansas. Post photos/videos of boards in action |
| **Craigslist** | Low | Still works in smaller metros |
| **Local bars & restaurants** | Medium | Demo a set, offer to sell or place on consignment |
| **Cornhole leagues/tournaments** | Medium | Show up with a set, hand out cards. Kansas has active leagues |
| **Word of mouth** | Low | Every set you sell is a demo unit. People will ask "where'd you get those?" |
| **Local craft fairs / farmers markets** | Medium | Summer weekends. Bring a set, let people play |
| **Church/school fundraiser events** | Low | Donate a raffle set = marketing |

**Local advantage:** No shipping costs, people can see/touch the boards before buying, you build reputation.

### Phase 2: Regional / Online

| Channel | Effort | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Etsy** | Medium | Great for custom/handmade goods. List the electronics kit first (cheap to ship) |
| **Amazon (FBA)** | High | Big reach but fees are steep (~15% + FBA fees). Better for electronics kits |
| **Your own website** | Medium | Shopify or similar. Keep it simple. Link from your GitHub repo |
| **YouTube build videos** | Medium-High | Film the build process. "How I Built LED Scoreboard Cornhole Boards" — drives traffic to your store |
| **Reddit r/Cornhole** | Low | Share the project, link to GitHub. Community loves DIY builds |
| **Instagram / TikTok** | Medium | Short videos of the boards in action at night = viral potential |

### Phase 3: Scale

| Channel | Effort | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Electronics kit on Amazon** | Medium | The real scale play — small, cheap to ship, good margins |
